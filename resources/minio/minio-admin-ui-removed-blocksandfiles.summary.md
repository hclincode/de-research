## Summary

Blocks & Files, an independent storage trade press publication, reported in June 2025 that MinIO removed GUI management features from its Community Edition and restricted them to the paid AIStor offering. The article documents user backlash on Reddit and community forums, with operators calling it a "bait and switch." This is the earliest major press coverage of MinIO's commercialisation pivot.

## Key Points

- **June 2025**: MinIO removed the full admin console from the community edition, leaving a bare-bones object browser.
- **Features moved to AIStor only**: User management, bucket policies, access control, lifecycle management, real-time monitoring, replication controls, LDAP/OIDC external identity provider integration.
- **Community reaction**: Users on Reddit and Hacker News described the change as a "bait and switch" for organizations that had deployed MinIO CE in production.
- **Strategic context**: The feature removal preceded by about six months the full maintenance mode declaration in December 2025.
- **Alternatives cited in user discussion**: Ceph, SeaweedFS, Garage as open-source alternatives; Cloudian as enterprise-grade S3-compatible storage.
- **AIStor pricing context** (approximate at time of reporting): ~$250k/PB/year public list price; minimum engagement ~$96k/year.
