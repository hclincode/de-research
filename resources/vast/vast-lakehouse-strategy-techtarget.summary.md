## Summary

TechTarget press article (2023) covering VAST Data's expansion into the data lakehouse market with the VAST DataBase product launch. Analysts quoted are skeptical that VAST can compete on database/analytics maturity; the article notes customers must own and operate their own data lake. VAST DataBase planned Spark, Trino, Parquet, Databricks, and Presto support. Article explicitly notes Weka "lacks a sophisticated storage stack" compared to VAST — an analyst quote, not a vendor claim.

## Key Points

- **Timeline**: VAST DataBase announced August 2023; DataEngine (global function engine) planned for 2024
- **Lakehouse strategy**: VAST positioned DataBase as "one scalable, transactional and analytical database management system ... integrated with your file and object storage system" — targeting both OLTP and OLAP
- **Spark/Trino support**: "DataBase will support Apache data importers Parquet, Spark and Trino alongside query interfaces such as Databricks, Presto, HPE Ezmeral and Vast's own REST API"
- **Analyst skepticism** (Steve McDowell, NAND Research): "Nobody understands your data better than your storage" — supportive framing
- **Analyst skepticism** (Scott Sinclair, ESG): "They can't be 20% better, they need to be two times better" — noting the challenge of competing in mature data management market
- **Operational burden**: "The problem is you own the data lake [with Vast]" — no managed service, unlike Snowflake/Databricks
- **Weka comparison**: Article notes Weka "lacks a sophisticated storage stack" compared to VAST (analyst quote)
- **Cost**: $0.02/GB on VAST NVMe vs $0.17/GB for Google Bigtable on SSDs (vendor claim, on-premise comparison)
